Kenya

Kenya is a beautiful country in East Africa with a wonderfully diverse range of wildlife. It provides a wealth of amazing sights and experiences to the hundreds of thousands of tourists who visit each year. However it is the home to some of the poorest people in the world. The juxtaposition of wealthy holiday-makers and poverty-stricken locals interacting is sobering and begs the question of why the highly lucrative tourism business is not supporting these people.

Maasai warrior

One reason for the persistant problem of inequality is that many of the assets used in the tourism trade are owned outside of the country. Safari vehicles and camps can be rented out to tourists and local guides are left with a tiny salary. This creates a poverty trap whereby the locals are unable to build a secure future for themselves and their families; and the (vast) proceeds of tourism are never seen by locals as they are exported immediately.

Maasai Mara national park

Fathom Beyond seeks to ameliorate the issue by keeping the proceeds of tourism in the local area so people can be empowered to build themselves a secure future and support their families.
